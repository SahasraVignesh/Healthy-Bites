Roasted Sweet Potato and Chickpea Buddha Bowl: Why this dish may help with Fatigue :
  • Sweet potatoes: Rich in complex carbohydrates, fibre, vitamins (especially vitamin A and C), and minerals like potassium and manganese, sweet potatoes provide sustained energy and support overall health.
  • Chickpeas: High in protein, fibre, and complex carbohydrates, chickpeas help stabilise blood sugar levels and provide lasting energy. They're also a good source of iron and folate, which are important for combating fatigue.
  • Quinoa or brown rice: Whole grains like quinoa and brown rice provide complex carbohydrates, fibre, and essential nutrients, offering sustained energy and promoting satiety.
  • Leafy greens and avocado: Packed with vitamins, minerals, fibre, and healthy fats, leafy greens and avocado support overall well-being and provide sustained energy.

    Ingredients:

  • 2 medium sweet potatoes, peeled and cubed
  • 1 can (15 oz) chickpeas, rinsed and drained
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 4 cups cooked quinoa or brown rice
  • 2 cups baby spinach or mixed greens
  • 1 avocado, sliced
  • 1/4 cup tahini (sesame paste)
  • 2 tablespoons lemon juice
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2-4 tablespoons water (as needed) Optional toppings: sliced cucumber, cherry tomatoes.

    Instructions:

  • Preheat your oven to 400°F (200°C). In a large bowl, toss the cubed sweet potatoes and chickpeas with olive oil, ground cumin, smoked paprika, salt, and pepper until evenly coated.
  • Spread the sweet potatoes and chickpeas in a single layer on a baking sheet lined with parchment paper. Roast in the preheated oven for 25-30 minutes, or until the sweet potatoes are tender and golden brown, stirring halfway through cooking.
  • While the sweet potatoes and chickpeas are roasting, prepare the tahini dressing. In a small bowl, whisk together tahini, lemon juice, minced garlic, and water until smooth. Add more water as needed to reach your desired consistency.Season with salt and pepper to taste.
  • To assemble the Buddha bowls, divide the cooked quinoa or brown rice among serving bowls. Top with roasted sweet potatoes and chickpeas, baby spinach or mixed greens, avocado slices, and any other desired toppings. Drizzle the tahini dressing over the Buddha bowls just before serving. Enjoy your nutritious and energising meal!
